Catholic Survey
Our website is dedicated to revival of the Tridentine Latin Mass, the pre-Vatican II Catholic Tradition that may be the key to Christianity's resurgence in the modern age.
     We acknowledge the many reasons why our churches forsook the Tridentine Mass after the Second Ecumenical Council of the Vatican, one of them being the relative inaccessibility of the Latin language itself. However, with technology, we can help Catholics warm up to the use of Latin in the liturgy of the Western Church.
     This survey will help us learn about our potential target audience, and help us create and organise our content that will impact the lives and faith of as many people as possible.
     May our Lord Jesus Christ bless you all.

Section Five: The Latin Language
The Tridentine Latin Mass is also called the Extraordinary Form of the Mass, and was the common form of the Mass celebrated before the Second Ecumenical Council of the Vatican (Vatican II).
     While the Council did not outlaw the use of Latin for Eucharistic gatherings (Holy Mass), it did allow Mass to be celebrated in the vernacular (local languages). This move has proven beneficial to specific communities not associated with European languages, however, it also has allowed for general decline in reverence for the Eucharist.
     One of the objectives of Ave Christus Rex - the admins having experienced both the Novus Ordo Mass (Mass in the vernacular post-Vatican II) and the Tridentine Latin Mass - is to make the latter popular again. We would like to know of your own experience with the Latin Mass.
